How the fuck did we live without these things.

I've had a handful of smartphones over the years.


Some good, some bad. Some became obsolete, others were destroyed and summarily discarded.


The probabilty that you will drop and destroy a touchscreen device is directly correlated with how much you love (or don't want to replace) said device.


My favorite phone was a Samsung S6 Active I purchased in late 2016. It featured a large battery and was water and drop resistant. The camera was better than I previously imagined possible. The screen was large and bright enough that I soon discovered that it was the first smartphone for which viewing pornography was actually and option.

I am not being paid to write this.

That phone performed admirably for almost three years. It servived dozens of drops onto hard surfaces. I threw it in my chalk bag while climbing. I zipped it in life jacket pocket on whitewater rafting trips for two summers. I nearly electrocuted myself and the phone shook it off. Once, I dropped it down into a pile of boulders on the ocean but was able to grasp and retrieve it unharmed.

I really liked that phone.
